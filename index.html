GET TO KNOW LYDIA.

I believe that Jesus Christ died for our sins on a cross, and rose up after 3 days. I believe that he is our God. I believe in God the father, son, and Holy Spirit.

church

Starting from when I was a baby, our family always attended the same church. Although, as the years went on, our church moved into a new building in 2018. Our service is split into two, we have one service for the Amharic-speaking Ethiopians, and we have an English congregation in another room as well. From 2020, until now, I’ve been leading worship through singing at my church called Meheret evangelical Church, we sing for the English youth congregation. My passion for singing started from when I was younger, when I would pretend to perform in front many people, and accept rewards, I used to practice speeches and sign autographs. I've always loved singing, but only sang at home. I was quite shy to sing at first. But slowly as I grew up, and got to really understand who God is and what he's done on the cross for our sins, I realized that God gave me this gift, so why would I use it on singing about relationships, when I can sing to king Jesus!! So I began to sing to the Lord through leading worship at church. Throughout the long process of singing, I've learned alot about the importance of ministry and prioritizations. I also teach the sunday school class, from grades 2-4.

culture.

background

I'm Ethiopian, raised in Canada by parents who migrated 22 years ago. Embracing my culture is vital; I speak Amharic, breaking the gap between generations. Even though my family knows English, speaking Amharic deepens our cultural connection. I've yet to visit Ethiopia but plan to soon. My Habesha friends enhance my cultural immersion—we communicate in Amharic and share relatable experiences. While I haven't mastered reading the Amharic alphabet, I'm eager to learn.

weddings

ethiopian weddings are my favourite to attend, in ethiopian weddings, the first wedding service is a traditional, western wedding and reception. but the next day, we celebrate something called a “melse” which is usually hosted the day after the wedding, and it's fully based on our culture. we all wear our cultural clothing, embrace our culture through foods, dancing, and worship. food

my favourite ethiopian dish is injera with kitfo and ibe, another highly favoured dish is cha cha tibs. growing up, my favourite dish was shiro, but that has fully changed as I began to get used to it. most ethiopian dishes are always served with injera, which is a flatbread that goes with all sauces.
